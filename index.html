Star Cluck.

During my graduate degree in 2009, I worked on a project called Star Cluck, which was a scrolling shooter game inspired by Star Fox. The game was developed using C++, DirectX 9, Visual Studio, and the Simple Academic Game Engine (SAGE). As part of the project, I made several contributions that helped enhance the overall gameplay experience such as level scripting, infinite terrain with terrain wrapping, spatial partitioning and triggers, collision detection with oriented bounding boxes. To ensure that the player's plane stayed within the game boundaries, I implemented a flight path that relied on collision with spatial partitions. Finally, I created a camera tether with a tolerance to provide a smooth and responsive player experience. Overall, my contributions to the Star Cluck project helped to create an enjoyable and engaging gaming experience for players.
